Where o4‑mini‑high stands today (May 2025)

  • Math logic: Formalises & verifies routines up to undergraduate numerical analysis; proof assistants need light steering.
  • Back‑end: Generates idiomatic FastAPI/Django services with Docker & CI; subtle transaction‑isolation bugs remain.
  • Web UI: Produces responsive Tailwind + React UIs; advanced custom hooks or offline handling still challenging.

Overall, a small team (1 senior engineer + designer) can ship a math‑oriented prototype but won’t trust it unattended.

Projection Reasoning

  • Model size vs. skill: 5–8× parameter growth per gen with better retrieval; math reasoning rises super‑linearly to o5, then tapers.
  • Tool integration: o5 introduces implicit agentic planning, removing most glue code.
  • Self‑debug loops: o6 pairs with live telemetry for autonomic SRE‑style remediation.

Human–AI Collaboration Shift

  1. o4: Human builds, AI accelerates.
  2. o5: AI builds, human reviews.
  3. o6: AI builds & operates, human specifies outcomes.

Implications for Teams

Startups (≤3 devs)

Today prototype faster; by o5 launch with almost no full‑time engineers; by o6 bottleneck shifts to market validation.

Enterprises

o5 enables legacy rewrites; o6 orchestrates brownfield + greenfield integration with automatic regression guards.

Education & hiring

Implementation skill gives way to specification skill—framing the right mathematical model.

Bottom line: o4 can deliver end‑to‑end demos but needs human hardening; o5 owns the MVP cycle with humans reviewing; o6 likely handles autonomous DevOps, letting domain experts ship full‑featured, mathematically sophisticated products solo.
